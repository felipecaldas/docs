# Choose Your Video Style

Once your script is ready, it’s time to define the **look and feel** of your video.  
Tabario allows you to select a visual **art style** that matches your story, tone, and audience.

Choosing the right style can transform your message — making it more emotional, playful, or cinematic.

---

## Available Video Styles

<Columns cols={3}>
  <Card
    title="Cinematic"
    icon="film"
  >
    A realistic and visually immersive style.  
    Ideal for professional campaigns, storytelling, and product showcases that need a polished, movie-like feel.
  </Card>

  <Card
    title="Crayon Drawing"
    icon="pencil"
  >
    A hand-drawn, artistic style.  
    Great for lighthearted, family-friendly, or educational content that benefits from a warm and approachable touch.
  </Card>

  <Card
    title="Anime"
    icon="wand-magic"
  >
    A stylized, expressive, and energetic style inspired by Japanese animation.  
    Perfect for creative brands, entertainment, or content targeting younger audiences.
  </Card>
</Columns>

---

## How to Select a Style

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Style Selection screen">
    After refining your script, click **“Next”** or **“Choose Style”** to open the style selection menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview available styles">
    Review sample thumbnails or short clips to understand how each style looks and feels.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your preferred style">
    Click on the style that best matches your brand or the mood of your video.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and proceed">
    Once selected, your choice will be used during video generation. You can return later to try a different style if desired.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Choosing the Right Style for Your Message

<Card title="When to Use Each Style" icon="lightbulb">
- **Cinematic:** Use for serious, emotional, or story-driven content.  
- **Crayon Drawing:** Use for approachable, fun, or educational videos.  
- **Anime:** Use for dynamic, bold, and energetic storytelling.  
</Card>

---

## Tips for Best Results

<Tip>
Match the **tone of your script** with your chosen style.  
For example, a professional script fits best with **Cinematic**, while a playful one pairs naturally with **Crayon Drawing** or **Anime**.
</Tip>
